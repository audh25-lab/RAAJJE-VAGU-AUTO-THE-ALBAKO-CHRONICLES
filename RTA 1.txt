# 1. GAME OVERVIEW

**Title:** Raajje Theft Auto 1  
**Genre:** Open-World Crime Simulation, Action-Adventure, Political Satire  
**Setting:** Maldives Archipelago (Malé, Hulhumalé, Addu City, Remote Atolls)  
**Time Period:** 2015-2025 (with historical flashbacks to 1900s)  
**Platform:** Cross-platform (PC, Mobile, Console)  
**Target Playtime:** 60-80 hours main story + endless free roam

## Core Concept

An epic crime saga following **Baokalo** (Ahmed Kipal Ronda), the black sheep of a respectable Addu family, as he rises from school troublemaker to gang enforcer to international crime lord to government asset, and finally leads a nationwide revolution to overthrow the corrupt president who betrayed him.

## Unique Features

- **125 Years of History:** Integrated real Maldivian history from 1900s to 2025
- **83 Gangs Network:** All real gang structures with authentic names and territories
- **Authentic Dhivehi:** Multiple dialects (Malé Bas, Addu Bas, Fuvahmulah Bas)
- **Family Drama:** Black sheep protagonist vs. golden child sister, reformed gang mother, respected musician grandfather
- **Political Satire:** Real scandals parodied (2012 coup, 2024 India boycott, Gang Act 2025)
- **Moral Complexity:** Multiple endings based on choices, family relationships, minor protection
- **International Scope:** From Maradhoo poverty to global crime networks (Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Afghanistan)

## Core Themes

1. **Paradise vs. Reality** - Tourism image hides narco-state dystopia
1. **Family vs. Ambition** - Destroying those who love you for power
1. **Cycles of Violence** - 1988 coup → 2012 coup → Your coup (2025)
1. **Black Sheep Redemption** - Can the family failure become the family savior?
1. **Small Nation, Big Corruption** - How 83 gangs control 500,000 people
1. **Islamist Extremism** - Religion weaponized for crime (ISIS recruitment, jihadi gangs)

# 2. HISTORICAL FOUNDATION (1900s-2025)

## Era 1: Sultanate to Independence (1900-1978)

### 1900-1931: Traditional Sultanate

- Maldives ruled by sultans under Islamic law
- Fishing and maritime trade economy
- Small-scale opium use in Royal Palace (16th century travelers documented)
- **1931:** First constitution drafted by Sultan Muhammad Shamsuddeen III
- **1932:** Constitution implemented, first Majlis formed

### 1953-1954: First Republic (Failed)

- Mohamed Amin Didi becomes first president
- Republic lasts only months before sultanate restoration
- Muhammad Farid Didi elected 84th Sultan

### 1959-1963: United Suvadive Republic

- Three southern atolls break away (Huvadhoo, Fuvahmulah, Addu)
- Abdullah Afif Didi leads secessionist movement
- Economic benefits from British military base on Gan Island
- **1962:** Ibrahim Nasir sends gunboats, crushes rebellion
- Afif flees to Seychelles
- **Game Connection:** Baokalo’s family witnessed southern rebellion

### 1965: Independence from Britain

- July 26, 1965: Full independence achieved
- British base on Gan remains until 1976

### 1968: Second Republic

- Ibrahim Nasir becomes first president (1968-1978)
- Sultanate permanently abolished
- **1972:** First tourists arrive, Kurumba resort opens
- **1977:** First hashish arrest (350 grams) - DRUG CRISIS BEGINS
- Law on Narcotics passed
- **1978:** Nasir flees to Singapore amid corruption allegations

## Era 2: Gayoom Dynasty (1978-2008)

### Maumoon Abdul Gayoom: 30-Year Rule

- **1978:** Takes power, rules until 2008
- Won 6 consecutive elections (sole candidate referendums)
- Islamic nationalism policy
- Tourism boom in 1980s-1990s brings prosperity and problems

### 1980 & 1983: Minor Coup Attempts

- Small, unorganized attempts against Gayoom
- Easily suppressed

### 1988: OPERATION CACTUS - Major Coup

- **November 3:** Abdullah Luthufi (businessman) + PLOTE Tamil mercenaries
- 80 armed mercenaries land in Malé before dawn
- Seize government buildings, TV/radio stations
- India launches Operation Cactus (9 hours response time)
- Indian paratroopers rescue Gayoom, defeat coup
- 19 killed including mercenaries
- Luthufi imprisoned 35 years (released 2023)
- **Game Connection:** Baokalo’s grandfather Mohamed Ronda witnesses chaos as young musician

### 1990s: DRUG EPIDEMIC EXPLODES

**1993: HEROIN ARRIVES**

- First heroin case detected, youth abuse escalates
- First major cocaine seizure (8kg at Malé Airport)
- **Early 1990s:** GANGS FORM - proliferation linked to heroin introduction
- Malé population explodes (180% growth 1995-2014)
- Youth unemployment reaches 25-40%

**Drug Routes Established:**

- **Golden Triangle/Crescent** proximity (Afghanistan, Pakistan, Iran)
- **Sri Lankan fishing vessels** (heroin via dhonis)
- **Trivandrum-Malé route** (primary corridor)
- **Velana International Airport** (luggage from India/Malaysia)
- **Southern atolls** (tuna boat hides, Gan/Addu drops)

**Gang Evolution (1990s-2000s):**

- 20-30 gangs in Malé by 2011
- Malé overcrowding: 103,693 people in tiny capital
- High divorce rates, family breakdown
- Unemployment + drugs = gang recruitment
- **Drug-Gang-Politics Symbiosis:** Politicians offer drugs/alcohol for crimes
- Members aged 12-25, exclusively male
- **Game Connection:** Rippoo (Baokalo’s mother) joins gangs 1993-1998 as female enforcer

**1997:** 1,372g hashish oil smuggled in corned beef cans from India

## Era 3: Democracy & Chaos (2008-2018)

### 2003-2004: Democratic Awakening

- **September 2003:** Prison riots after inmate Hassan Evan Naseem tortured to death in Maafushi Prison
- His mother exposes torture, triggers protests
- Mohamed Nasheed (exiled journalist, imprisoned 16 times) founds MDP in 2003
- **August 2004: “BLACK FRIDAY”** - 5,000 protesters, largest in history
- Demand Gayoom’s resignation after 26 years
- NSS uses tear gas, batons on civilians - 22 hours of violence
- **Game Connection:** Baokalo age 4, earliest memory is chaos and screaming
- **December 2004:** Tsunami devastates Maldives, sets development back 20 years
- **2005:** Parliament votes for multi-party politics

### 2008: First Democratic Election

- **October:** Nasheed defeats Gayoom (first democratic president)
- Promises carbon-neutral Maldives by 2019
- 2009: Underwater cabinet meeting on climate change
- Liberalizes tourism (local guesthouses allowed)
- BUT: Opposition in judiciary/parliament loyal to Gayoom
- **Game Connection:** Baokalo age 8, watches election with hope

### 2010s: Gang-Politics Fusion

- **Gangs become political enforcers** for campaign cash
- Islamist radicalization grows (Salafism from Saudi Arabia)
- Al-Qaeda active before 2008 elections
- **2014: Salafi-Jihadi + Gang Fusion** confirmed
- Gang leaders act as vigilante religious police
- Politicians use gangs for violence, intimidation

### 2012: THE COUP AGAINST NASHEED

- **January 2012:** Nasheed arrests Criminal Court Judge Abdulla Mohamed (alleged Gayoom bias/corruption)
- **February 1-6:** 22 days of protests, police join opposition
- **February 7, 2012: FORCED RESIGNATION**
- Police/military mutiny, corner Nasheed at army HQ
- Nasheed resigns to avoid bloodshed
- Mohamed Waheed Hassan (VP, Gayoom ally) becomes president
- Nasheed calls it coup d’état; official inquiry says “voluntary”
- **Game Connection:** Baokalo age 12, learns **politics = violence = power**

### 2013-2018: Yameen Dictatorship

- **September 2013:** Election - Nasheed wins plurality but not majority
- **November 2013:** Abdulla Yameen (Gayoom’s half-brother) wins narrowly
- **2015:** Nasheed arrested on terrorism charges for 2012 judge arrest
- **March 2015:** 13-year sentence after closed trial (no defense counsel)
- UN calls it “vastly unfair, arbitrary, disproportionate”
- **2016:** Nasheed granted UK medical leave, flees, gets asylum
- **Game Connection:** Baokalo joins gangs 2015, age 15, during Yameen peak

**Yameen Era Abuses:**

- Jails opposition leaders
- Stuffs judiciary with loyalists
- $80 million corruption scandal
- Chinese mega-projects (debt trap)
- Stifles media, threatens journalists
- Gang-politician links deepen

### 2014-2017: ISIS RECRUITMENT PEAK

- **250 Maldivians join ISIS** (highest per capita in world)
- Radicalization in prisons: gangs + extremists alliance
- Extremists recruit addicted gang members, promise redemption via jihad
- Justify crimes: “stealing from kafirs for jihad is halal”
- **Ahmed Rilwan (journalist) disappearance** - gang/extremist murder
- **Yamin Rasheed (blogger) murder 2017** - stabbed 16 times for exposing extremism
- **May 2021: Nasheed assassination attempt** - IED outside home, critically injured
- **Game Connection:** Baokalo witnesses journalist murders, learns media = dangerous

### February 2018: Supreme Court Crisis

- **Feb 1:** Supreme Court orders 12 opposition MPs reinstated, political prisoners released
- Orders Nasheed’s release, overturns convictions
- **Feb 4:** Yameen declares state of emergency
- Arrests 2 Supreme Court justices on “coup plot” charges
- 45-day emergency rule
- **Game Connection:** Chaos creates opportunity for Baokalo’s gang operations

### September 2018: Democratic Victory

- Ibrahim Mohamed Solih (Nasheed’s childhood friend) wins landslide
- 58% vs Yameen’s 42%, 89% voter turnout
- Yameen concedes, then tries to challenge (court rejects)
- November 2018: Solih sworn in, Nasheed returns from exile

## Era 4: Modern Day (2018-2025)

### 2019-2023: Solih Presidency

- **May 2019:** Nasheed elected Parliament Speaker (unanimous)
- Corruption probes against Yameen (money laundering conviction, later overturned)
- India strengthens ties: $2 billion aid, infrastructure projects
- **Gang problem persists:**
  - 44-45 active gangs in Malé
  - 12 in Addu City
  - 26 in remote atolls
  - **Total: 83 gangs**
  - 8 “highly active” gangs monitored daily by police
  - 97 child members (aged 12-13) documented
  - 32 gang-related deaths
- **Drug statistics:** 7,500+ users (2013), doubled to ~14,000 by 2021
- Major busts: 300-355kg heroin/cocaine from tuna boats, Sri Lankan vessels

### 2023: Muizzu Takes Power

- Mohamed Muizzu (PNC, pro-China) wins presidency
- Nasheed resigns as Speaker November 2023 (impeachment threat)
- **Muizzu’s “No Gangs” Denial:**
  - **2025 Gang Act (#7)** passed despite claiming “no gangs exist”
  - PNC MPs Abdulla Rifau, Mohamed Musthafa claim “gangs don’t operate”
  - Police confirm 83 gangs: 44-45 Malé, 12 Addu, 26 atolls
  - ~3,050+ identified members
  - Act gives police sweeping powers: 48hr detention without lawyer, warrantless searches
- **Game Connection:** Baokalo becomes government asset 2024, betrayed 2025

### Political Corruption (Current 2025)

- **Suspended Deputies:** Mariyam Shiuna, Malsha Shareef, Mahzoom Majid (inflammatory statements)
- **Gang-PNC Links:** Extortion rackets in Hulhumalé residential zones
- **Ahmed Muaz Networks:** Islamist co-optation of gangs continues
- **Drug photo scandals:** Ali Ihusaan (Homeland Security Minister)
- **Land fraud:** PNC-linked resort deals
- **Money laundering:** Moosa Zameer (Finance Minister)
- **Arms smuggling:** Mohamed Ghassan Maumoon (Defense Minister)
- **International drug ties:** Dr. Abdulla Khaleel (Foreign Affairs)

# 7. DHIVEHI LANGUAGE & SLANG GUIDE

## Regional Dialects

### MALÉ BAS (Capital Dialect - Standard)

**Characteristics:**

- Most common, official contexts
- Softer sounds, less archaic
- **Example:** “Fannu” (rope)
- Used by government, media, education

**In-Game:** Most NPCs speak this

### ADDU BAS (Southern Dialect - Baokalo’s Home)

**Characteristics:**

- Most archaic and distinct
- Retroflex ‘ṇ’ sound preserved (Ṇaviyani ޱ)
- Replaces final sounds
- **Example:** “Fanno” instead of “Fannu”
- Sounds “country” to Malé residents

**In-Game:**

- Baokalo speaks this natively
- Malé gangs sometimes mock him: “Kaley Addu tha?” (You’re Addu?)
- Code-switches to English when frustrated

### FUVAHMULAH BAS (Mulaku Bas)

**Characteristics:**

- Word-final ‘l’ sound unique
- **Example:** “Gedharo” (home, like Sinhalese)
- Poetic, rhythmic to other Maldivians

**In-Game:** NPCs in Fuvahmulah atoll missions

### HUVADHU BAS

**Characteristics:**

- Most use of retroflex ‘Ţ’ sound
- Retains old Sinhala words
- Closest to original Maldivian linguistically

**In-Game:** Remote atoll NPCs, elderly characters

-----

## Curse Words & Insults (REAL DHIVEHI)

**Extremely Offensive:**

- **Sodu** - Bastard, most common curse
- **Hoshifa** - Strong insulting curse
- **Thilhen** - “You” but extremely offensive form
- **Kaley** - “You” very informal, disrespectful to strangers

**Usage In-Game:**

- Gang fights: “Kaley sodu!”
- Rivals: “Thilhen vaahaka eh dhakkaabalaa!” (I won’t talk to you - extremely rude)
- Insults: “Maa sodu kuda ibilaa” (Don’t talk like a bastard)

## Pronouns (Respect Levels)

**Formal (Politicians, Elders):**

- **Aharen** - I (formal)
- **Alhugandakah** - I (very formal, to superiors)
- **Thibeyfulhaa** - You (respectful, to elders/bosses)

**Standard:**

- **Mashakah** - Me (standard)
- **Ahannakee** - I am (standard)

**Disrespectful (Gang Usage):**

- **Kaley** - You (disrespectful)
- **Thilhen** - You (extremely offensive)

**In-Game Examples:**
